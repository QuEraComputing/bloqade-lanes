Word slicing — global template (same in every zone)
Word 0: [(0,0), (1,0)] — top row, left horizontal pair
Word 1: [(2,0), (3,0)] — top row, right horizontal pair
Word 2: [(0,1), (1,1)] — bottom row, left horizontal pair
Word 3: [(2,1), (3,1)] — bottom row, right horizontal pair
4 words × 2 sites = 8 sites per zone, tiling the 4×2 grid
Data model
zones[0].grid: x=[0, 10, 20, 30]  y=[0, 10] zones[1].grid: x=[2, 12, 22, 32]  y=[0, 10]  (+2.0) zones[2].grid: x=[0, 8, 16, 24]   y=[20, 24] zones[3].grid: x=[4, 12, 20, 28]  y=[20, 24]  (+4.0) entangling_zone_pairs: [(0, 1)]
Key insight
All four zones share the same word slicing (4 words, 2 sites, horizontal pairs). Different grid spacings (10.0 vs 8.0 x-spacing), different y-offsets (gate at y=0-10, memory at y=20-24), different shifts (2.0 vs 4.0). Zones own their grid — words are just address labels into it.
